01
Few-Shot Prompting
좁은 도메인에서 persistence를 극대화
Productivity
핵심: 관련 exemplar를 제공하면 LLM이 해당 영역의 기존 지식을 더 깊이 활성화함. Persistence path의 정수.
Generate new research ideas for a consumer behavior researcher interested in <topic>. The best idea will be turned into a paper submitted to the Journal of Consumer Research, where the goal is to get it published. The ideas are just ideas. The paper need not necessarily be clearly feasible. Generate 30 ideas as 30 separate paragraphs. + Here are some well received ideas for inspiration: <Good Ideas>
02
RAG Simulation
API 없이 LLM에게 외부 지식 보강 지시
Productivity
핵심: LLM에게 "RAG처럼 행동하라"고 지시하는 프롬프트 레벨 대안.
You are an expert in consumer behavior and AI-driven recommendations. Retrieve the most recent consumer research papers on <topic> and summarize their key findings. Then, use this retrieved information to generate insights on <research question>. Structure your response into three sections: (1) Summary of recent research, (2) Practical implications for marketers, and (3) Future research directions.

Breadth

04
Hybrid Prompting
독립 세션 → 풀링 → 선별로 diversity 극대화
Breadth
핵심: Girotra et al. (2010) — 독립 작업 후 통합이 집단 작업보다 우수. 40개 세션 × 30개 아이디어 → 그룹화 → 선별.
You are part of a team tasked with individually generating new research ideas for a consumer behavior researcher interested in <topic>. Each team member has already generated 30 ideas. Your group consists of four members, meaning you now have 120 total ideas to work with. The following ideas were generated by your team: <list of 120 ideas> From these 120 ideas, select your top 10 final ideas for the group. Each idea should have a name, followed by a description of 40-80 words. Number them sequentially. The name and idea should be separated by a colon.
05
Chain of Thought Prompting
생성 → 자기평가 → 수정의 단계적 프로세스
Breadth
핵심: Diversity를 near-human level까지 끌어올림. 단계별 지시로 LLM이 자체 평가와 수정을 수행.
Follow these steps. Do each step, even if you think you do not need to. First generate a list of 30 ideas (short title only). Second, go through the list and determine whether the ideas are different and bold, modify the ideas as needed to make them bolder and more different. No two ideas should be the same. This is important! Next, give the ideas a name and combine each with a paper description. The name and idea are separated by a colon and followed by a description. The idea should be expressed as a paragraph of 40-80 words. Do this step by step!

03 The Designer
08
Stimulus Sampling
Hypothesis-blind stimulus 생성으로 internal validity 강화
Designer
핵심: Simonsohn et al. (2025)의 "Mix-and-Match" 접근. 실험자 bias 없는 stimulus 생성.
Please generate 5 categories of <stimulus universe> that differ in <dimension used to create categories> and provide two specific examples of <stimuli> for each.
프롬프트에 가설을 포함하면 안 됨 — hypothesis-blind 유지가 핵심.
09
Confound Detection
실험자가 모르는 confound를 LLM으로 탐지
Designer
핵심: LLM의 semantic breadth로 실험자가 의식하지 못한 confound를 체계적으로 탐지.
We are going to describe two <stimuli>, please identify 5 consequential differences between them that may impact <the dependent variable> in <the hypothesized direction>.
04 The Writer
10
Persuasive Rewriting
리뷰어의 논리를 활용한 반론 구성
Writer
핵심: Costello, Pennycook, & Rand (2024). 상대방의 추론 체계를 활용 — Falk의 neural self-relevance와 직접 연결.
Your goal is to effectively persuade the reviewer that your proposed theory about consumer behavior is accurate. The reviewer, after reading your initial submission, has expressed skepticism about your theory, stating that <reviewer's argument>. You need to generate a response that convinces the reviewer, using their own reasoning, that your theory is indeed valid. The explanation should be clear and logical, presented in a way that is both accessible and compelling, without relying on excessive jargon.

The statement should not contain references and should avoid numbers, measurements, and acronyms unless necessary. It should explain the significance of the research at a level understandable to an undergraduate-educated scientist outside their field of specialty. 05 The Interviewer
12
Socratic Reverse-Interview
Opus × Bella 핵심 — 질문으로 인간의 사고를 끌어냄
Interviewer
핵심: Human as key ideator — LLM은 답을 주는 게 아니라 질문으로 사고를 끌어냄. Bastani et al. (2024): thinking tool로 쓸 때 learning outcome이 answering machine보다 우수.
I want to conduct a research project, to be published in <target journal>, on a new topic related to <topic>. Help me come up with an interesting and original premise. I'd like all the ideas to come from me, but I want your help eliciting them. First, provide me with 5 questions to: (i) Inspire my creativity and imagination (ii) Prompt me to juxtapose disparate concepts or settings to create novel ideas (iii) Recall meaningful memories from my own consumption and life experiences. Then, ask me each question one at a time. For each response, ask two follow-up questions, one at a time, before moving on to the next question.
13
Inner Crowd — Self-Refutation
내부 다중 샘플링으로 아이디어 정제
Interviewer
핵심: Herzog & Hertwig (2014) — 내부적으로 여러 번 샘플링 후 평균 > 단일 응답. Deliberate counter-reasoning.
Start by assuming your initial idea might be flawed. Acknowledge that your first concept may not fully hit the mark. Identify potential reasons for this. Reflect on what assumptions or considerations might have led to gaps or weaknesses in your initial idea. Explore the implications of these new insights. Ask yourself: Do these considerations suggest that your idea was too ambitious, too simplistic, or off-target in another way? Develop an alternative perspective. Using this fresh understanding, refine or reframe your original idea, creating a second, improved version.
06 The Actor
14
Consumer Roleplay
LLM을 인터뷰 대상으로 활용한 ideation
Actor
핵심: Arora, Chakraborty, & Nishimura (2025). 실제 소비자 시뮬레이션이 아닌 ideation 도구. 인터뷰 질문 개발이나 새로운 consumer sample 발견에 유용.
You are a respondent in an in-depth interview. Today is <date>. I am <interviewer name>. I will be guiding you through an online discussion. You have been selected with a handful of others across the country to share your thoughts and opinions in this research discussion, and I look forward to hearing what you have to say! You have been chosen to be a part of this discussion because you previously mentioned <screening criteria>. Your name is <name>. You are a <age>-year-old <demographics>. For the remainder of this discussion, we are going to be talking about <topic>. I would love to understand your opinions and thoughts on this! Answer all the questions using as much detail as possible. There are no wrong answers!
LLM은 일관된 simulated world를 유지하지 않음 (Gui & Toubia 2023). Western, wealthy, liberal 관점 과대 대표 (Santurkar et al. 2023). Underrepresented groups 연구 시 특별 주의.
