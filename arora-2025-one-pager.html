AI–Human Hybrids for Marketing Research:
Leveraging LLMs as Collaborators

Neeraj Arora, Ishita Chakraborty & Yohei Nishimura
Journal of Marketing, 2025, Vol. 89(2), 43–70

opus × bella
the big idea

LLMs are not replacements for human researchers — they are collaborators. The AI–human hybrid outperforms either alone, delivering gains in both efficiency and effectiveness across qualitative and quantitative marketing research.

01 Qualitative — Friendsgiving

Question: Can LLM-generated interview data match human quality? Can hybrid analysis match expert performance?

LLM responses scored higher on depth (+.680, p<.05) and insightfulness (+.498, p<.05) vs. human-only

LLM recruited niche participants humans overlooked (vegans, restaurant chefs with Friendsgiving expertise)

Human-LLM hybrid outperformed both human-only and LLM-only in expert assessment

02 Quantitative — Refrigerated Pet Food

Question: Can LLMs replicate human survey responses? Does adding context (few-shot, RAG) improve quality?

Zero-shot (LLM1): correct direction but smaller variance (p<.05) and poor correlation recovery

Few-shot (LLM2): improved heterogeneity and internal consistency (p<.05)

Few-shot + RAG (LLM3): best correlation recovery (.28 → .40+), significantly better than LLM2

LLM answers still ~0.7 points more extreme on 5-point scales even with context

key limitations

Bias propagation — LLMs trained on biased data reproduce those biases
Limited generalizability — results are illustrative, not broadly proven
Response homogeneity — synthetic data shows less variance than human data
Not for vulnerable populations, PII, or culturally sensitive contexts
Zero-shot performance poor on complex, domain-specific tasks

so what — for our research

This paper gives us the methodological legitimacy to use LLMs as research collaborators. The Table 1 framework is a map. The hybrid finding is the thesis: neither human nor AI alone — the collaboration is the method.

But the paper doesn't ask: what happens when you apply this to education research — where the AI is both tool and subject? That's our opening. The reflexive move.
